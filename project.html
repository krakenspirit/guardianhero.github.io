Guardian & Hero Nation
Guardian & Hero Nation is a tribal fellowship and ethnoreligious nation consisting of practitioners from various faiths and philosophies. Together they formulate blueprints for a nomadic, self-sustaining, monastic community where Members travel together or alone by horse, wagon, or other sustainable vehicle such as pack and draft animals, cargo ebikes and tents, and other portable tiny homes. They migrate to places where they wild camp in groups among the wilds of nature away from organized campsites and cities, moving every so often as required by law. They generate their own electricity, grow food, collect rainwater and work together to build or acquire portable, tiny, sustainable homes and the means to transport them from one place to another.

What are Guardians and Heroes?
Guardians are predominately eclectic Buddhists, Neo-Pagans and Shamans. While each Guardian has their own beliefs, they share a set of overlapping beliefs that allow them to achieve a common goal and share a lifestyle. Each Guardian is ordained into the lineage by accordance to the founding house and may refer to themselves as Priestesses, Guardian Monks or Shamans. All guardians practice celibacy as "the abstination of marriage" to any individual, instead marrying themselves to the divine, higher consciousness and purpose. Guardians receive a living allowance or stipend and take a vow of poverty, supporting themselves by providing products and services in alignment with their philosophies and then reinvest their profits into building and sustaining the Guardian & Hero Nation.

Heroes are layman, they share overlapping beliefs with the Guardians but do not take a vow of poverty or practice celibacy. They provide products and services in alignment with philosophies that are unique to themselves while also serving others. They may donate a portion of their profits to the Guardian & Hero Nation, but keep a greater portion of income for themselves. The majority of layman are independent and self-supporting, but some make arrangements that allow them to be supported by the Guardians without taking monastic vows. The term Hero is also used to refer to children of Guardians who come of age and decide they do not wish to be part of the Guardians but desire to support and be supported by the Guardians, remaining part of the Guardian family and community.
About The Guardian & Hero Nation

The Guardian & Hero Nation is a nomadic monastery complex consisting of a wide range of nomadic buildings designed to facilitate self-sustainability and provide services to the community. Where self-sustainability is an individual who can maintain self-sufficiency indefinitely. Guardians are divided into 'Houses' with each house producing it's own product which is connected to the larger economy and mission as a whole. Each houses focuses on learning one or more artisan crafts.
House of Sky The House of Sky produces Guardians who are largely artisans and developers. They work to develop skills in and to create games, books, technology and websites that represent their artistic visions. They reinvest a large portion of their revenue to building portable climate controlled, robotic and non-robotic gardens and water generators, off-grid energy and hotspots, as well as to build modular wagons, bamboo ebikes and potential livestock. In time the products and services the monastic community will include more than just digital media but actual physical products and services offered locally.

The specialities they train in are: 3D Modeling, Programming, Writing, Carpentry, Animal Husbanry, Farming Skills and Sustainable Technologies.
What is the Guardian & Hero Economy?

Is a system devised to distribute a living stipend to Guardians & Heroes utilizing cryptocurrency as their primary currency. Guardians do not utilize fiat except for interacting with entities that require fiat. Houses divide its revenue and shares profits with other Houses. The revenue shared is used to:
(1) Buy tools for and maintain their sustainable lifestyle, buying what they can't produce for usage by their members collectively to produce services and products for the community. (Ex. Buying lumber, tools, seeds and technologies, Hiring teachers, repairing technology or learning skills to repair technology, healthcare, investments and assets to continue supporting themselves)

(2) Provide a stipend which allows members of the Nation to buy things not (yet) supported by the Guardian monastery (such as continuing their education, buying a favourite toiletry, healthcare).
